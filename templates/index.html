Algorithmic Trading

See for yourself.

Algorithmic trading, also known as algo trading, is a method of executing trades using pre-programmed instructions or algorithms. These algorithms are designed to automatically buy or sell assets based on predefined criteria, such as price, timing, and volume, without human intervention. By leveraging the power of advanced mathematics and computational technology, algorithmic trading can analyze large amounts of data and execute trades at speeds and frequencies that are impossible for a human trader. This approach helps traders capitalize on market inefficiencies, reduce the impact of emotions on trading decisions, and potentially enhance overall trading efficiency and profitability.

MVT Architecture ML Model

MVT (Model-View-Template) is Django's architectural pattern, similar to MVC but tailored for Python.

  • Model: Handles the data layer and business logic.
  • View: Processes requests, interacts with the Model, and sends data to the Template.
  • Loose Coupling: MVT promotes loose coupling between components, meaning changes in one component (like the Model) have minimal impact on others (like the Template).
  • Reusable Components: The clear separation of responsibilities makes it easier to reuse components like Models or Templates across different parts of the application.
  • Simplified Testing: Since the Model, View, and Template are separate, each can be tested independently, simplifying the testing process.
  • Template: Renders data into HTML for the user interface.
